Satvik Ingredients

Grains & Cereals

Grains & Cereals – Energy & Digestion

  • Rice – Easily digestible, provides quick energy, cooling for the body.
  • Whole Wheat – Rich in fiber, aids digestion, and provides sustained energy.
  • Millets (Ragi, Jowar, Bajra, etc.) – High in fiber, regulates blood sugar, and boosts immunity.
  • Barley (Jau) – Detoxifies the body, helps in weight loss, and improves digestion.
  • Poha (Flattened Rice) – Light on digestion, rich in iron, and provides quick energy.

Uses: Khichdi, pulao, plain rice, kheer, roti, puri, upma, halwa, dosa, porridge

Pulses & Legumes

Pulses & Legumes – Protein & Muscle Growth

  • Moong Dal – Easiest dal to digest, rich in protein, good for gut health.
  • Masoor Dal – High in iron, helps in blood formation, and boosts metabolism.
  • Toor Dal – Good source of protein, supports heart health.
  • Chana Dal – Rich in fiber and protein, supports digestion.
  • Urad Dal – Strengthens bones and nervous system, improves digestion.

Uses: Dal, khichdi, dosa, soups, sambhar, chilla, idli

Dairy Products

Dairy Products – Strength, Immunity & Brain Health

  • Milk – Strengthens bones, nourishes the brain, and provides energy.
  • Curd (Dahi) – Aids digestion, cools the body, and supports gut bacteria.
  • Ghee – Boosts brain function, lubricates joints, enhances digestion.
  • Paneer (Cottage Cheese) – Rich in protein and calcium, good for muscle building.

Uses: Kheer, sweets, herbal drinks, buttermilk, kadhi, raita, cooking, tempering, Ayurvedic remedies, subzis

Satvik Utensils

Traditional utensils that enhance the purity and nutritional value of your food

Clay Pots (Earthenware)

Why Used?

  • Natural and porous, allowing slow, even cooking.
  • Enhances the natural taste of food.
  • Retains the prana (life energy) in food.

Health Benefits

  • Preserves essential minerals like iron, calcium, magnesium.
  • Alkalizes food, balancing pH levels and reducing acidity.
  • Aids digestion and improves gut health.

Best Used For

  • Khichdi (Rice and moong dal cooked with mild spices)
  • Sabzi (Mixed vegetables cooked with minimal spices)
  • Dahi (Curd) & Buttermilk (Clay pots help retain natural probiotics)
  • Dal (Lentil Soup)
    • (Slow-cooked for better taste and nutrition)

Brass (Pital) Utensils

Why Used?

  • Retains heat well and enhances flavor.
  • Reacts with tamarind and acidic foods, so requires a tin coating (kalai).

Health Benefits

  • Rich in zinc and copper, which boost immunity.
  • Helps in the absorption of iron from food.
  • Improves skin health and digestion.

Best Used For

  • Rice (Cooking rice in brass utensils gives it a unique taste)
  • Doodh Haldi (Turmeric Milk) (Brass vessels help enhance medicinal properties)
  • Kheer (Rice Pudding) (Slow-cooked for a rich flavor)

Bronze (Kansa) Utensils

Why Used?

  • Traditionally used for eating food.
  • Kills harmful microbes and bacteria.
  • Considered spiritually pure in Ayurveda.

Health Benefits

  • Enhances gut health and improves digestion.
  • Reduces acidity and detoxifies the body.
  • Improves hemoglobin levels.

Best Used For

  • Chapati, Sabzi, Dal, and Rice served on a kansa plate
  • Kansa glasses for drinking water or milk

Iron or Cast Iron Kadai & Tava

Why Used?

  • Naturally fortifies food with iron.
  • Makes food tastier and enhances texture.

Health Benefits

  • Prevents anemia by increasing hemoglobin.
  • Strengthens bones and muscles.
  • Improves energy levels.

Best Used For

  • Roti / Chapati (Made on an iron tava enhances iron intake)
  • Saag (Leafy Greens) (Spinach, methi, or mustard greens cooked in an iron kadai)
  • Upma / Poha (Cooked in an iron pan for a wholesome breakfast)

Wooden Utensils

Why Used?

  • Does not react with food, unlike metal or plastic.
  • Gentle on cookware and does not scratch surfaces.

Health Benefits

  • Maintains the purity of food by preventing chemical reactions.
  • Preserves the natural taste and nutrients in food.

Best Used For

  • Churning buttermilk (Mathani – wooden churner) for natural probiotics
  • Mixing atta (dough) in a wooden bowl for soft chapatis
  • Stirring and serving food

Stone Silbatta (Grinding Stone)

Why Used?

  • Used for grinding fresh spices, chutneys, and masalas.
  • Avoids heat generation, unlike electric mixers.

Health Benefits

  • Retains essential oils and nutrients lost in electric grinding.
  • Enhances digestion and improves metabolism.

Best Used For

  • Fresh Coconut Chutney
  • Homemade Masalas (Spice Blends)
  • Ginger & Tulsi Paste for Herbal Teas

Banana Leaves or Leaf Plates

Why Used?

  • Considered spiritually pure and used in temples.
  • Imparts a mild natural flavor to food.

Health Benefits

  • Contains polyphenols (antioxidants) that boost immunity.
  • Helps digestion and detoxifies the body.

Best Used For

  • Traditional Saatvik Thali (Dal, rice, sabzi, roti, chutney, and sweets served on banana leaves)
  • Prasadam / Bhog offerings

Utensils to Avoid

These utensils may compromise the purity and health benefits of your Satvik food

Aluminum

Leaches harmful chemicals into food, affecting the brain and digestion.

Non-Stick (Teflon Coated)

Releases toxic fumes when heated.

Plastic & Melamine

Contains BPA and other chemicals that can harm the body.

Microwave Utensils

Alters the molecular structure of food, reducing its sattvic qualities.

About Satvik Aahar

Satvik Aahar is a traditional dietary philosophy that emphasizes pure, wholesome, and naturally grown foods that promote physical health, mental clarity, and spiritual well-being.

This diet consists of foods that are fresh, nutritious, and prepared with minimally processed ingredients. It avoids foods that are overly spicy, bitter, sour, or stale, and excludes meat, fish, eggs, onions, and garlic.

By following a Satvik diet, you nourish not just your body but also your mind and spirit, creating harmony and balance in your overall being.
